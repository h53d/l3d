Privacy Policy

How Lite3D handles your JT files and device data.

Effective Date: July 01, 2026
Lite3D: JT File Viewer values your privacy.

This policy explains how your data is handled when you use the app. We do not collect it. Your JT files and engineering data stay on your device.

Policy

1. No Data Collection

Lite3D: JT File Viewer does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to us or third-party analytics services. Viewing, inspection, and export happen locally on your device.

  • No user accounts or sign-in
  • No analytics, advertising, or tracking
  • No cloud uploads of JT files or derived meshes
  • No data sharing with us or others

Your JT assemblies, screenshots, recordings, and exports remain under your control unless you choose to share them yourself.

2. Offline Processing

JT import, product-structure inspection, viewport rendering, and STL/GLB export run entirely offline on your Mac, iPhone, iPad, or Apple Vision device. We do not upload your files to any server.

3. Local Storage Only

Opened JT files are processed in memory and on your local device. Lite3D may also store a local recent-files list and, when you grant folder access for multi-file assemblies, security-scoped bookmarks so repeat opens can access sibling part files without sending data off-device. These records stay in your app sandbox and are not transmitted to us.

4. No Third-Party SDKs

We do not use advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or third-party data services. No tracking. No fingerprinting. No hidden network calls for viewer functionality.

5. Your Rights

Because we do not collect personal data, there is nothing for you to request, delete, or revoke from us. Your intellectual property and CAD visualization data remain yours alone. You can clear recent-file history from within the app at any time.

6. Changes to the Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Significant changes will be noted by updating the effective date at the top of this policy.
